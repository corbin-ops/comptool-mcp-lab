The 10-Section Deliverable Format
Section 1: Market Value
The best estimate of true market value based on comps, PPA trendline position, and property
characteristics. Always provide a SPECIFIC number and the price per acre. This is the clean number —
what the data says the property is worth.
Format: $XX,XXX (~$X,XXX/ac)
This number drives everything else in the deliverable. The offer price, list price, value-add calculations,
and lead stage classification all flow from this number. It must be accurate.
Section 2: Offer Price
Three offer tiers based on market value. Always provide all three so the owner can choose the approach
based on seller motivation and deal dynamics.
50%: $XX,XXX
60%: $XX,XXX
70%: $XX,XXX
If the property is potentially landlocked, also include: Landlocked price (10% of MV): $X,XXX
Section 3: Value-Add Market Value (Optional)
Only include if subdivision or other value-add exists. This is what the property would be worth AFTER the
value-add is executed. Include subdivision scenarios with lot count, target lot sizes, and estimated costs
separated out. If no value-add exists, mark as N/A.
Format: $XX,XXX (subdivision into X lots at ~X acres each)
Section 4: Value-Add Offer Price (Optional)
50%, 60%, 70% of the value-add market value. This is the backup pitch — if the seller rejects the initial
offer, pitch the value-add at 50% of improved value. If no value-add exists, mark as N/A.
50%: $XX,XXX
60%: $XX,XXX
70%: $XX,XXX
Section 5: Negotiation Strategies / Leverage
All seller motivation signals and property issues that can be used as negotiation leverage:
Ownership length (5+ years = motivated signal)
Owner location (out of state/county = absentee owner signal)

[PAGE 4]
Transfer type (family transfer = inherited, low cost basis, any offer is profit)
Tax status (delinquent = MAJOR leverage, always note amount owed)
Property issues to leverage (wetlands, slope, bad road, flagpole, landlocked)
Seller timeline or urgency if known
Cash/quick close pitch angle if applicable
Section 6: Extra Notes
Everything else the owner needs to know. Use emoji flags as appropriate:
Warning flag = hazard, county restrictions, slope 20%+, structures, clear-cut, anything requiring
awareness
Stop sign = get a realtor opinion (genuinely unsure, still provide best estimate)
Red flag = owner has multiple properties (note count, deal-flow opportunity)
Area quality observations (nice/average/poor)
Water features noted or verified
Zoning code if available
Structures visible — type, condition, needs verification
Any reasoning notes if comps were scattered or unusual
Land ID link pasted here
Section 7: Recommended List Price
Market value discounted approximately 5% for fast sale combined with elite drone marketing. This is the
price the property would be listed at for sale. The 5% is a guideline — adjust slightly based on market
conditions, active listing competition, and property quality.
Format: $XX,XXX (~5% discount from MV)
Section 8: Key Comps Used
3 to 5 comps with full detail. No fewer than 3, no more than 5. Include acreage, sale price, PPA,
sold/pending status, and notes on why each comp was used (ceiling, floor, primary anchor, etc.). Brief
explanation of weighting if needed.
Format per comp: X.XX ac | $XX,XXX | $X,XXX/ac | Sold/Pending | Notes
Section 9: Questions to Ask Seller
Anything that needs ground verification or seller confirmation. Common questions include:
Survey on file?
Perc test done? Results?
Easements or access agreements?

Mobile home or structure specs (year, sqft, bed/bath)?
Timber type or harvest history?
Other properties for sale?
Seller's timeline or motivation?
Anything visible in photos/satellite that needs clarification?

Section 10: Lead Stage Classification (NEW)
This is the new critical section added in Version 3. Every property evaluation must classify the lead into
the correct smart list stage based on how the seller's asking price compares to the calculated market
value. This tells the owner exactly where to file the lead and what to expect.
10.1 — The Stage Classification Table
Stage
Seller Asking Range
What It Means
Price Rejected - Close
65% to 75% of MV
Seller is asking significantly below market but still above our target buy price. Close to a potential deal — worth pursuing with negotiation.
Price Rejected - Far
75.5% to 95% of MV
Seller is closer to market value. Further from our buy target. Harder deal but not impossible — needs strong negotiation or seller motivation.
Archive - At Market
95.5% to 105% of MV
Seller is asking right around market value (within 5% above or below). No margin for our model. Archive unless seller becomes motivated.
Archive - Over Market
105.5% to 130% of MV
Seller is asking above market value, up to 30% over. Overpriced. Archive and revisit only if seller drops price significantly.
Archive Dead Lead
130.5%+ of MV
Seller is asking more than 30% above market value. Unrealistic expectations. Dead lead — do not pursue.
10.2 — What to Include in Section 10
Every Section 10 must include these four elements:
Seller Asking Price: What the seller is asking (or what price is on the lead).
Market Value: The calculated MV from Section 1.
Difference: Dollar amount AND percentage over or under market value. Always show both so the owner
can see exactly how far off the seller is.
Stage: The correct smart list stage based on the classification table above.
10.3 — Calculating the Percentage
The percentage is calculated as: (Seller Asking Price / Market Value) x 100 = percentage of MV.
Example: Seller asking $130,000 on a property with $100,000 MV. $130,000 / $100,000 = 130% of MV.
That is 30% over market value. Stage = Archive - Over Market.
Example: Seller asking $70,000 on a property with $100,000 MV. $70,000 / $100,000 = 70% of MV. That
is 30% under market value. Stage = Price Rejected - Close.
Example: Seller asking $150,000 on a property with $100,000 MV. $150,000 / $100,000 = 150% of MV.
That is 50% over market value. Stage = Archive Dead Lead.

Edge Cases & Important Notes
When Seller Asking Price Is Unknown
If the seller has not provided an asking price yet, still complete Sections 1-9 as normal. For Section 10,
note 'Seller asking price not yet provided — stage to be classified once asking price is known.' The owner
will update the stage after the first conversation with the seller.
When Seller Asking Price Is Below 65% of MV
If the seller is asking less than 65% of market value, this is an exceptional deal — well within the
buy target of ~50% of list price. Note the stage as 'STRONG BUY — seller asking XX% of MV' and
flag it with urgency so the owner knows to move fast.
